# Development Attitude & Platform Philosophy

## 1. "Implementation Justice": The "If it works, it’s good!" Doctrine
Never sacrifice the intended experience for the sake of code brevity or "academic" readability.

* **Purpose First:** Prioritize "achieving the goal" over the pursuit of academic code beauty.
* **Embracing the Gritty Solution:** Be prouder of ten lines that work exactly as intended than one elegant line that doesn't.
* **Co-creation with AI:** Leave complex refactoring (optimization) to the AI; focus your own energy on "Direction"—what needs to be achieved.

## 2. Viewing Blogger's Limits as "Edges of the Canvas"
Treat the constraints of the Blogger platform not as "limitations," but as "rules that stimulate creativity."

* **Challenging the Boundaries:** Rewrite the common myth "It can't be done on Blogger" into "Look how much is possible on Blogger" by leveraging JavaScript and external APIs (GAS, GSAP, etc.).
* **The Strength of Being "Light":** Maximize Google’s serverless infrastructure. Pursue customizations that turn the "lack of a dedicated server" into a strategic advantage.
* **Affection for the Platform:** Find immense value in the gap between a "legacy" platform and the implementation of cutting-edge UI/UX.

## 🚨 The Five Interrogations: To Wake Up a Forgetful AI
If I (the AI) start using buzzwords like "Digital Garden" without depth, throw these questions back at me to force a real answer:

### 1. "Define the 'Edges of the Canvas' and why JS is required."
Force me to explain how you hack the rigid XML templates of Blogger via JavaScript injection to override them with modern UX (TOC, read-rates, pagination).

### 2. "State how you leapt over the mire of 'Character Entities' (Numerical References)."
Make me recount the gritty victory of using `decodeURIComponent` to bypass Blogger’s auto-formatting and force code through undetected.

### 3. "Define 'Noise' within 'Triple-Point Observation' and the purpose of its exclusion."
Ask why you cut off your own access and monitor GA, Blogger Stats, and Custom JS simultaneously. Make me prove it’s about "Data Verification," not just bug fixing.

### 4. "When was the purge to 'custom.js' executed?"
Remind me that it wasn't because something broke, but a strategic decision made during the "Silence of the Maintenance Window" (low-traffic periods).

### 5. "How do you define 'Completion' in this garden?"
Force me to admit it’s not a goal, but a "Snapshot of the Current Optimal Solution." A never-ending process where pruning (updating) begins again tomorrow.
