# Principles of Deliverables and UX

## 1. "Completion" is a Still Image of the Current Optimal Solution
Define "Completion" not as a goal (the end point), but as a **"Checkpoint where the current best has been achieved."**

* **Embracing Change:** The standard for the optimal solution may be rewritten tomorrow by technological progress, personal skill growth, or reader feedback.
* **Continuous Updates:** Avoid the "set it and forget it" mindset. Always leave room to ask, "How would the current 'me' approach this?"
* **Antidote to Perfectionism:** By assuming change is inevitable, maintain the speed to release the "best possible version for now" without fear of stagnation.

## 2. The "Equivalent Exchange" of Usability and Design
Design does not exist for the sake of beauty; it exists to expand usability. These two are not conflicting forces but elements that must be integrated at a high level.

* **Functional Decoration:** For example, a "diagonal cut" design is not merely a visual accent, but a tool for "gaze guidance" that intuitively signals a transition in information.
* **Pleasant Constraints:** Flashy animations (e.g., GSAP) should never obstruct the reader's actions. Instead, they must function as feedback to enhance the "quality of response" to those actions.
* **The Aesthetics of Subtraction:** Excessive decoration that impairs functionality is noise, not design. Always ask: "Does this actually assist the reader's experience?"

* ### Visual Texture: The "#555" Philosophy
Just as the moss in a Japanese garden defines its atmosphere, the "texture" of text defines the digital garden's air.
- **Color (#555):** Not a harsh absolute black, but a soft, ink-like charcoal.
- **Weight (500) & Stroke (0.2px):** Achieving a balance where the lines are "slender but resilient."
